# 1.1 Context and Motivation

## The Documentation Problem in Experimental Science

Modern experimental research faces a persistent challenge that is rarely addressed in training: the lack of structured documentation practices. Researchers are taught to design and execute experiments, but the rigorous recording of those experiments is rarely treated as a discipline in itself.

The consequences are well documented. Experimental insights are lost when a student finishes their degree. Procedures cannot be reproduced because critical parameters were never recorded. Institutional knowledge dissolves over time, leaving research groups repeating work already done by a predecessor.

As Bernadette Longo (2000) explains, documentation has always functioned as a tool to stabilize scientific knowledge within an organization. In modern research environments, this requires a Single Source of Truth (SSoT): a centralized, reliable structure where information is not merely stored, but engineered to be accessible, traceable and permanent.

## The IMB-CNM Context

The Institute of Microelectronics of Barcelona - National Center for Microelectronics (IMB-CNM, CSIC) is the largest research centre in Spain dedicated to micro and nanotechnologies. It hosts the largest clean room facilities in the country with full capability to process its own CMOS technologies.

In this environment, the cost of poor documentation is especially high. Experiments are expensive, equipment is shared and processes are highly iterative. A small undocumented change in a process parameter, such as furnace temperature or photoresist concentration, can propagate throughout an entire fabrication run without anyone knowing when or why it was introduced.

## Survey: Confirming the Gap

During the first weeks of the project, conversations with researchers and students revealed recurring frustrations about documentation. A structured survey was designed and distributed across research groups at the UAB Campus.

A total of 113 responses were collected. Most respondents reported their current practices were insufficient for ensuring reproducibility and traceability. Full results are presented in Chapter 3.

## Origin: The RACO Project and Write the Docs 2025

This project is motivated by the RACO project (estRucturació i Automatització del COneixement), an internal IMB-CNM initiative led by Esteve Farrés Berenguer aimed at consolidating research knowledge and documentation practices at institutional level.

IMB-CNM has recently initiated complementary institutional actions that demonstrate a deliberate shift from informal documentation practices towards a more structured approach. A Data Management training programme has been organised in collaboration with La Salle URL University, addressed to junior PhD students. RACO operates as the internal methodological counterpart, exploring how Docs-as-Code principles can be applied consistently across real research projects.

The conceptual framework of this project is also influenced by the Documentation as Code (DaC) philosophy, as presented at the Write the Docs Berlin 2025 conference. The talks from this edition were reviewed during the literature research phase and directly shaped several design decisions described in Chapter 2.

One of the goals of this project is to contribute material that Esteve Farrés Berenguer would like to present at Write the Docs Berlin 2026, where he aims to share the evolution of the RACO project with the international community.

## From Writing Reports to Managing Knowledge

During the development of this project, it became clear that the main challenge was not producing reports, but preserving the information generated throughout a research project. Experimental decisions, unsuccessful tests, parameter adjustments, and practical observations are often not recorded at all, making them difficult to recover months later.

For this reason, the focus shifted from document creation towards knowledge management. The objective was not simply to produce better documents, but to establish a practical method for organising information so that it remains accessible, understandable, and reusable over time.

In this framework, documentation is treated as a research asset that evolves together with the project. The following chapters describe the design, implementation, and validation of this framework at IMB-CNM.
